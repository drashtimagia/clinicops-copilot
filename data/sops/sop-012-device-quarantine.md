# SOP-012: The "Three Strikes" Escalation Rule
**Effective Date:** March 01, 2024
**Applies To:** All clinical operations staff, Floor Managers

## 1. Purpose
To prevent "alert fatigue" and dangerous equipment wear due to repeated reset attempts by clinical staff.

## 2. Policy
The "Three Strikes" rule applies to any electromechanical device on the floor (monitors, infusion pumps, centrifuges, autoclaves, etc).

1. **Strike One**: If a device fails (e.g., error code down, battery failure, or heating issue), attempt standard troubleshooting per its manual (e.g., restart, replace battery). Log the result.
2. **Strike Two**: If the same device fails again for the same issue within a 7-day period, repeat troubleshooting and log a warning.
3. **Strike Three**: If the device fails a third time within a 7-day period for the same issue, it is considered unsafe for patient care and clinical operations.

## 3. Action Required
Upon the Third Strike:
1. Immediately remove the device from service.
2. Tag the device with a red "DO NOT USE - VENDOR ESCALATION" tag.
3. Systematically escalate the issue past internal Biomedical teams directly to External Vendor Support for replacement or heavy maintanence. Attempting a fourth reset is a fireable safety violation.
