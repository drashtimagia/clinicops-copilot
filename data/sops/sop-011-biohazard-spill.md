# SOP-011: Biohazard Spill Recovery
**Effective Date:** January 15, 2023
**Applies To:** All clinical lab staff, Biomeds

## 1. Purpose
To safely recover from biohazard material spills occurring within internally sealed or mechanical laboratory equipment (e.g., centrifuges, incubators) where standard surface cleaning is insufficient.

## 2. Procedure
1. **Immediate Reaction**: Power off the device immediately. Do not open the enclosure if an aerosolized risk exists. Wait 30 minutes for particles to settle.
2. **Quarantine**: Place a highly visible 'Biohazard Spill - DO NOT OPEN' sign on the device.
3. **Escalation Phase 1**: Contact Environmental Services (EVS) to perform a secondary decontamination on the exterior of the device and immediate surrounding work area.
4. **Escalation Phase 2**: Once EVS clears the area, log a ticket for Biomedical Engineering to perform the deep internal cleaning. Note: Clinical staff should never dismantle laboratory equipment to clean internal spills due to the risk of exposure and mechanical injury.

## 3. Memory & Tracking
All biohazard spills inside devices must be logged into the incident tracking system with the tag `#BiohazardSpill` to track equipment reliability and recurrent seal failures.
