# ECG Machine E200 — Quick Service Manual

## Device Overview
The ECG Machine E200 records cardiac electrical signals and prints or displays waveform traces for diagnostic review.

## Common Use Areas
- ER
- ICU
- Cardiology
- Room 101
- Room 102

## Common Symptoms
- No signal detected
- Poor waveform quality
- Paper not feeding
- Device freezes during test
- Leads not recognized
- Battery or power issue

## Error Codes
### E201 — Lead Off
One or more patient leads are disconnected.

**Action**
1. Reattach all leads securely.
2. Check skin contact and electrode adhesion.
3. Replace worn electrodes if needed.

### E202 — Poor Signal Quality
Electrical noise or weak contact is affecting reading.

**Action**
1. Reposition electrodes.
2. Reduce patient movement.
3. Check lead cable damage.
4. Retry reading.

### E203 — Printer Error
Internal thermal printer cannot feed or print correctly.

**Action**
1. Check paper roll placement.
2. Remove jammed paper.
3. Reload approved ECG paper.
4. Restart printer function.

### E204 — Storage Full
Internal memory limit reached.

**Action**
1. Export or delete older records if permitted.
2. Restart device.
3. Retry test.

### E205 — Battery Fault
Battery cannot sustain normal operation.

**Action**
1. Connect to AC power.
2. Confirm charging icon appears.
3. Escalate if battery warning repeats.

## Troubleshooting

### No ECG Trace Appears
1. Verify leads are connected.
2. Check electrode placement.
3. Replace dry or loose electrodes.
4. Restart the test.

### Screen Freezes
1. Save current session if possible.
2. Restart the device.
3. If freeze repeats, quarantine device.

### Printer Not Working
1. Confirm paper is loaded correctly.
2. Check for paper jam.
3. Reboot unit.
4. Escalate if printer remains inoperable.

### Weak or Noisy Signal
1. Ask patient to remain still.
2. Reapply electrodes.
3. Inspect cables for cracks or loose contact.
4. Retry reading.

## Safety Notes
- Do not rely on distorted traces for diagnosis.
- Replace damaged leads immediately.
- Escalate repeated signal or printer faults.

## Maintenance
- Clean lead connectors daily
- Replace worn cables as needed
- Check printer feed weekly
- Battery test monthly
